Sprocket Information


Most major chainsaw equipment distributors recommend changing your sprocket every one to two chains. With a centre drive sprocket set-up you only need to replace the ‘rim’ whereas a spur sprocket requires the drum and attached spur sprocket to be replaced at the same time.

Jono & Johno specialises in centre-drive sprockets, though we can source most spur sprockets if required.




Modern chainsaws run two different types of sprockets; spur sprockets and centre drive sprockets. There are hundreds of variations of each of these types of sprockets.

A spur sprocket consists of a star shaped sprocket that is moulded into the sprocket drum. The sprocket drum is placed on top of the chainsaw clutch.

A centre drive sprocket consists of two separate components; a “sprocket rim” and a “sprocket drum”.

A centre drive sprocket is a more economical set-up than a spur sprocket. There is no reduction in quality when converting from one to the other.
